Glucose

The process for calculating glucose levels using a glucometer typically involves the following steps:


* Wash your hands with soap and warm water to ensure they are clean and free from any residue that could affect the accuracy of the reading.
* Insert a test strip into the glucometer according to the manufacturer's instructions.
* Use a lancing device to prick the side of your fingertip. You may also use an alternate site such as your forearm or palm, depending on the instructions    provided by your healthcare provider.
* Squeeze your finger gently to produce a small drop of blood and place it on the end of the test strip.
* Wait for the glucometer to display your blood glucose reading. The amount of time this takes will depend on the specific glucometer model you are    using.
* Record your blood glucose reading in a logbook or digital device, along with the date and time of the test.
   It's important to note that the process for calculating glucose levels using a glucometer may vary slightly depending on the specific model you are    using.Always follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully and consult with your healthcare provider if you have any questions or concerns.

Diabetes Pedigree Value

A diabetes pedigree is a tool used by healthcare providers to assess the risk of developing diabetes in individuals who have a family history of the disease. The pedigree value is a number assigned to each family member based on their relationship to the proband (the person who is the focus of the pedigree).
Diabetes pedigree Calculator is available in our system, just enter number of relatives effected by diabetes in consideration of corresponding relation with them.



BMI

BMI stands for Body Mass Index and is a measure of a person's body fat based on their weight and height. It is commonly used as a screening tool to identify whether a person is underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.
Diabetes pedigree Calculator is available in our system, just enter the height in cm and weight in kg.
